Teriyaki

    Teriyaki Sauce:

  • 1 knob of ginger
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • 1 cup of cooking sherry
  • 1 cup of mirin
  • 1 cup of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 4 tablespoon water
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame seeds

    • Meat

  • 1 lb of Chicken thighs(I prefer the skin to be on)
  • Salt and pepper

Teriyaki Instructions:

    Making the sauce

  1. Mince the Galic and Ginger.
  2. Add 1 tbsp of oil in a sauce pan. Then, add your garlic and ginger. Cook until the two become fragrent.
  3. Now, add your cooking sherry, mirin, and soy sauce. Bring to a boil for about 5 minutes on medium heat.
  4. Once it had reduce by 1/3 make you slurry of cornstarch and water. Then, drizzle into the sauce to thicken.
  5. Sesame seeds are optional but you can add them now if you wish.

    Cooking the Chicken

  1. Dry off the chicken then salt and pepper them. Let sit for 30 minutes. Overnight is preferred.
  2. In a cold pan, place your chicken in with the skin side down. This will render the fat and the chicken skin crispy.
  3. Once the chicken skin is golden brown flip the chicken over and do the same thing on that side.
  4. When the chicken reaches 165 degrees the chicken is cooked. Now dunk your chicken in the teriyaki sauce.
  5. Pro tip: I let the chicken sit in the sauce for couple minutes to let the sauce get in all the crevices.

    6. Enjoy! and be happy!
