Fat and Happy

    Meats

  • 1 pack of Andouille saugsage
  • 1 1/2 lbs of cooked shredded chicken

    • Roux

  • 1 Cup of oil
  • 1 Cup of Flour

    • Vegetables

  • 1 Cup of Celery
  • 1 tbsp Galic
  • 1 Green and Red bell pepper
  • 1 Cup of Onion

    • Spices

  • 2 Bay leaves
  • 1/2 tsp Thyme, dried
  • 4 cups Chicken broth
  • 1 tsp Black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp Cayenne pepper
  • 1 tsp Kosher salt
  • 1 1/2 tsp Paprika

Instructions:

Tip: prep your vegetables first by cutting dicing them. Same with your meats.

  1. First! You are going to make your roux. You are going to put you pot on a medium low heat and add your cup of oil. After the oil begins to smoke a little you are going to add your flour.Then, you are going to whisk. This is going to take some time but you're going to keep on whisking your roux until it become a milk chocolate color. DON'T BURN YOUR ROUX!
  2. When your roux is a chocolate color you are going to add your vegetables(Celery, Onion, and Bell peppers) in the roux and let them sweat. When the onions become translucent your are going to add your garlic. Cook it for a little bit long but don't burn your garlic.
  3. Add your chicken broth to the pot. Then, add your spices and meats.
  4. When the pot starts to bubble turn down the heat to just above a simmer. This is going to cook for a couple hours now and every 20 minutes you'll stir the pot. If you want shrimp throw them in for the last 20 minutes.
  5. Once the gumbo is done cooking. serve over rice.

Enjoy! and be happy!
